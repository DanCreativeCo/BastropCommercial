Bastrop County has quietly become one of the most strategically positioned
markets in Central Texas. The eastward expansion of the Austin metro — anchored
by Austin-Bergstrom International Airport, Tesla's Gigafactory, and SpaceX's
Boring/Starlink operations — has pushed industrial and logistics demand straight
into Bastrop's path.

## What's driving demand

- **Highway access.** State Highway 71 connects Bastrop directly to Austin and
  the airport, making it a natural logistics and manufacturing corridor.
- **Land availability.** Unlike landlocked Travis County, Bastrop still offers
  large, contiguous, developable tracts at a fraction of Austin pricing.
- **Pro-development posture.** Local zoning and economic-development incentives
  remain favorable to industrial and commercial users.

## What it means for investors

Contiguous, properly zoned industrial acreage on the corridor is the scarcest —
and most appreciating — asset class in the county. Land-banking today against a
multi-year absorption curve is the thesis driving the most sophisticated buyers
in this market.
